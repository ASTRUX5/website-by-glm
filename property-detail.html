A home that holds centuries

This heritage villa sits on the eastern shore of Dal Lake, where the morning light first touches the water. Built in the early 1900s, it carries the weight of a hundred winters and the warmth of a thousand evenings by the fireplace.

Every room faces the lake. Every window frames the Zabarwan mountains differently — sometimes silver with snow, sometimes purple with storm clouds, always changing, never the same twice.

The walnut wood interiors have aged like fine wine. The hand-carved ceilings tell stories their craftsmen never wrote down. This isn't just a house. It's a living piece of Kashmir.
